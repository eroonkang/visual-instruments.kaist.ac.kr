시각적 형식과 구조, 경험과 문화를 구성하는 도구와 체계

Machines that shape visual form, structure, experience, and culture

시각도구연구실은 시각적 형태의 생산을 ‘요소의 배열’이 아닌 ‘구조의 설계’로 접근합니다. 이미지, 언어, 데이터가 어떻게 구성되고 작동하는지를 탐구하며, 이를 위한 새로운 시각 체계의 건축을 실험합니다.

디자인과 코드, 자동화의 경계가 흐려진 환경은 새로운 시각 문해력을 요구합니다. 우리는 기계와 함께 작업하고, 사람과 기계를 위한 형식을 함께 만들어가는 현재와 미래에 걸쳐, 이러한 기술적 디자인 도구가 시각문화의 생산과 소비를 어떻게 바꾸는지 질문합니다.

KAIST 산업디자인학과에 기반을 둔 시각도구연구실은 그래픽 디자인, 시스템 사고, 컴퓨테이셔널 미학의 교차점에서 활동하며, 시각적 구조를 더 잘 보이게 하고, 더 잘 쓰이게 하며, 더 비판적으로 사고하게 만드는 도구를 설계하고 개발합니다.

The Visual Machines Lab explores how visual elements—image, language, and data—are not just arranged but architected. We investigate how visual output is constructed, encoded, and interpreted, experimenting with the tools and systems that make it possible.

As design merges with code and automation mediates visual culture, new approaches to visual literacy become necessary. What does it mean to design with machines—or for them? How do software tools shape what we see, how we understand, and how meaning is made?

Based in the Department of Industrial Design at KAIST, the Visual Machines Lab operates at the intersection of graphic design, systems thinking, and computational aesthetics—building visual frameworks that are legible, functional, and critically informed.

디렉터

강이룬, 산업디자인학과 부교수

디자이너이자 교육자로서, 교육 프로그램과 스튜디오를 만들어오며 기술 환경에서 디자인의 역할 변화를 탐구해 왔다.

미국 캘리포니아예술대학과 뉴욕 파슨스디자인스쿨에서 그래픽디자인 및 커뮤니케이션디자인 학과장을 역임했으며, 디자인 스튜디오 매스프랙티스를 운영하며 Google, MIT 건축학과, UCLA 건축도시디자인대학 등을 비롯한 다양한 문화·예술·기술·교육기관과 협업해왔다. 또한 디자인 리서치 콜렉티브 908A를 공동 설립하여 Getty Research Institute, Smithsonian 등 주요 예술기관과 함께 프로젝트를 진행했다. TED Senior Fellow로 선정되었고, MIT SENSEable City Lab에서는 특별 연구원으로 활동했다.

LACMA Art+Technology Lab의 첫 번째 지원 프로젝트로 작업이 선정되었고, 뉴욕현대미술관 (MoMA), 서울시립미술관, 시애틀공공도서관 등에 작품을 전시했으며, 미디어시티서울, 서울도시건축비엔날레, 베니스건축비엔날레 등에 참여했다.

현재는 ‘지시의 층위로서의 디자인’을 디자인 비평과 생산의 프레임으로 삼아 연구를 이어가고 있다.

Director

E Roon Kang, Associate Professor

Design educator and creative director with a background in building programs and studios that explore the evolving role of design in technological contexts.

Before joining KAIST, he served as Chair of Graphic Design at California College of the Arts and as Director of the BFA Communication Design program at Parsons School of Design. He previously led Math Practice, an award-winning design studio that collaborated with partners including Google, MIT Architecture, and UCLA AUD, and co-founded 908A, a research collective developing alternative design tools in collaboration with institutions such as the Getty Research Institute and the Smithsonian. He is a TED Senior Fellow and a former research fellow at MIT’s SENSEable City Lab.

His work has been selected as an inaugural project of LACMA’s Art+Technology Lab, and has been exhibited at MoMA, the Seoul Museum of Art, and the Seattle Public Library, as well as featured in the Seoul Biennale of Architecture & Urbanism and the Venice Biennale of Architecture.

His current research focuses on design as layers of instructions—an evolving framework for both critique and production.

Join the lab

VML welcomes collaboration with researchers and students from diverse backgrounds. For details on the admissions process, timeline, and required documents, please refer to the KAIST Admissions and Industrial Design Department’s FAQ.

PhD students

We invite applications from individuals whose experiences and capabilities align with the lab’s research focus. Before submitting a formal application, please reach out via email with your CV, cover letter, and portfolio.

Master’s students

Graduate students in KAIST’s Department of Industrial Design are both students and researchers. They take coursework while actively participating in research within an assigned lab. Students are admitted for both Spring and Fall, and the lab selection takes place after admission. VML looks forward to working with students who bring diverse perspectives.
